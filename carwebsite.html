Jaguar F-Type SVR

Something very telling of F-Type customers: Apparently some owners of the previous F-Type felt the car was too quiet at lower speeds. And so the $129,000 F-Type SVR was designed to strike fear at red lights with a more amplified growl. But the noise isn’t all show. With a top speed of 200 mile per hour, this is Jaguar’s very competent effort to throw down in the supercar market.

Mercedes Benz E43 AMG

When the new E-Class debuted in January, the sedan brought with it semi-autonomous drive capabilities found in higher-end Benz models–you could simply keep your hands on the wheel and it would handle itself on the highway. But when you ramp up the engine to a a twin-turbo 3-liter V6 that cranks out 396 horsepower and offers a driver-tunable air suspension, you might actually want to take the wheel. While this is no E63 (which puts out 577 hp), the E43, like many of Benz’s AMG models, is still a wolf in sheep’s clothing.

Porsche Boxster 718 S

This is the first new Porsche to use a four-cylinder turbo boxer engine. It’s named after the four-banger, mid-engined racers from the 1950s and ’60s. THis new Boxster starts at a friendly $56,000 and will be available around June. Not bad. The “Lava Orange” paint will cost you an extra $2850, however.

Lincoln Navigator Concept

While a lot of the design elements of this Navigator will show up in next year’s new model, don’t get too excited about the gullwing doors. They won’t appear on the production model (but there’s always the Tesla Model X if you desire a crossover with such). The most talked about concept car at the New York International Auto Show is a glimpse at what Lincoln hopes to do next with their retro-American,luxe living-room-on-wheels–catch up with the Cadillac Escalade. Perhaps the most thoughtful feature: an intercom system that lets those exiled in the last row to communicate with those up front.

Audi Q7 2017

Three rows, long-distance comfort, and panache are all expected in this class, but the Q7 adds another: agility. The base engine is a 252-hp 2.0-liter turbo four; a 333-hp 3.0-liter supercharged V-6 is optional. Both engines pair with an eight-speed automatic and all-wheel drive; available four-wheel steering and an air suspension help the Q7 remain composed. The high-quality interior features the latest in infotainment, including the optional Virtual Cockpit, a 12.3-inch driver-facing cluster.
